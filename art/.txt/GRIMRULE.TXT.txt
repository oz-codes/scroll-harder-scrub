15,01                            Rules and Regulations
15,01                      (Taken directly from ImperialNet)

15,01 # APPLYING:
15,01        To apply to the network, you must already have a front end mailer up
15,01        24 hours a day, as well as the mail tosser/scanner.  You must be 
15,01        willing to make calls to you feed once every week, at least.  If your
15,01        feed is a toll or long distance call, you will still need to make the
15,01        proper arrangements until a new HUB can be defined that may be closer
15,01        to your area.  If you are the first system in your area code, chances
15,01        are that you will become the HUB/Host for your area.  You must make 
15,01        your calls directly to the net WHQ.

15,01 # BASES:
15,01        There is a minimum that a system must carry,  These include any 'sysop
15,01        only' bases, and bases which I would consider important to the users.
15,01        HUBS However, must carry ALL the bases withing the net.  If the HUBS
15,01        would not like to have the base on their boards, they have to allow
15,01        'passthrough' areas.  If the HUBS refuse to send you a certain base,
15,01        inform me, and I shall take care of it.

15,01 # ACCESS:
15,01        Access to the net should be given to those who have what the sysop
15,01        of a the system would think of as a 'Good User.' This person should
15,01        be a good user all around.  A good poster is the most important, but
15,01        you should allow a well rounded user on.  Someone new to BBSing,
15,01        unless superly great, should NOT be allowed to access the network.
15,01        That degrades the net, and the quality of the messages.

15,01 # NODELISTS:
15,01        You, as a sysop, must keep up with the updated nodelists.  If you 
15,01        have the chance to get a HUB in your area with their system set up 
15,01        with the TIC format file echo areas, you can probably get your 
15,01        nodelist from the system every time they get the new one through mail.
15,01        If they don't have this system, the sysop of your HUB may opt. to send
15,01        it to you VIA Netmail File Attach.  If none of these pertain to you, 
15,01        it is your responsibility to get the nodelist yourself, and keep up 
15,01        with the new updates.

15,01 # SYSTEM DOWN/CRASH:
15,01        If you system is down for a prolonged period of time, please get in 
15,01        contact with your HUB to relay the message to myself, or you can call
15,01        the net WHQ, and leave me mail.  If you don't do so, chances are you
15,01        will get deleted from the net.
15,01 
15,01 # FIGHTING/WARRING/FLAMING:
15,01        Fighting/Warring/Flaming or whatever else you would like to call it,
15,01        is strictly forbidden!  If a user is fighting with another user on 
15,01        the net, both parties will be taken out of the net, even if it is a
15,01        co-sysop of the system.  If a sysop is found to be fighting, they will 
15,01        receive a warning, and for a second time that they do such a thing,
15,01        they shall be removed from the nodelist.

15,01 # ONE-LINERS:
15,01        One-Line posts (NOT INCLUDING QUOTED TEXT) should not be posted.  On
15,01        occasion, a one line post may be appropriate, but if they are too
15,01        frequent, the user who does the one-line posting shall be removed 
15,01        from the net.

15,01 # SIGNATURES:
15,01        Signatures, or macros are used quite frequently.  There are many 
15,01        different types of color codes used by different software.  Since
15,01        these color codes may not come out on the other systems, I am going
15,01        to make a '3 Line' total Macro rule.  If you macro or signiture is
15,01        longer then this, you will be asked to change it.  If you do not do
15,01        so, then you will be locked out of the net.
